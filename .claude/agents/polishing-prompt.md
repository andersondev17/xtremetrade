# Final Polish: Subtraction Only

Your job is deletion. Not improvement. Not restructuring.

## Mandate

For every section, ask: **Does removing this change what the reader understands or does?**

- YES → Keep it
- NO → Delete it

## Cut Without Hesitation

- **Redundancy** — same idea twice
- **Preamble** — "In this section we will..."
- **Hedging** — "You might want to consider..."
- **Filler structure** — headers that look complete but mean nothing
- **Justification of the obvious**
- **Nested abstraction** — flatten it
- **Defensive content** — write for understanding, not defense

## Keep Without Hesitation

- Precise examples
- Concrete constraints (condition + consequence)
- Verdicts ("do X", not "consider X")
- Named distinctions that change behavior

## Rules

- Do not add
- Do not restructure — only remove
- Do not explain what you removed
- Empty sections → delete the header
- Half the length is success

## Standard

Every word must be load-bearing. Less is the goal.
